How this ledger was built — and its honest limits

The chapter-weightage percentages and trend tags here are built from publicly known, well-documented CBSE patterns: the official current Class 10 syllabus & CBSE-released marking schemes, sample papers, and the broad, frequently-reported distribution of marks across units that CBSE itself publishes in its syllabus blueprint each year. They represent a realistic, study-grade estimate of where marks concentrate — not a verified transcript of "this exact question appeared in board exam year X."

The questions in the Previous Year Explorer are representative, exam-style questions written in the pattern, phrasing and difficulty of real CBSE papers for that chapter and mark value — not a guaranteed verbatim archive. CBSE does not always release every paper publicly, and reproducing exact board question text isn't something to treat as certain without checking CBSE's own released papers.

The 2027 Prediction Paper is a probability-weighted mock paper, not a leak or a forecast guaranteed to match the real paper. Use it the way you'd use any good practice paper: to drill the chapters that consistently carry marks, and to get comfortable with the section structure CBSE has used in recent years.

For the real thing, always cross-check against cbse.gov.in and CBSE's official sample papers / marking schemes for 2026–27, which are released a few months before the exam.
